Boston Crime: When Danger Peaks & Where It Lurks

An interactive data story exploring temporal rhythms and spatial patterns in Boston crime incidents, using the Analyze Boston Crime Incident Reports API.

CS617 Spring 2026 — Assignment 3

CHART 1 / When Does Crime Strike?

This heatmap reveals the pulse of crime across the week. Each cell encodes the number of incidents at a given day-and-hour combination. Darker colors mean more danger. Hover over any cell to see the exact count.

Key Insight: Crime peaks between 12 PM – midnight, especially on Friday and Saturday evenings. The 2–5 AM window is consistently quiet across every day of the week.

CHART 2 / What Crimes Hit Which Districts?

Not all neighborhoods face the same threats. This grouped bar chart breaks down the top 5 offense categories across Boston's police districts, revealing which areas are hit hardest and by what type of crime.

Key Insight: Larceny dominates nearly every district, but districts like B2 (Roxbury) and B3 (Mattapan) show elevated assault and drug violation rates compared to downtown districts like A1 and D4.

How These Charts Connect

Chart 1 tells us when to be alert — Friday and Saturday evenings are peak danger windows. Chart 2 tells us where and what — certain districts face disproportionate levels of specific crimes. Together, they paint a picture of targeted resource allocation: deploy more officers to high-crime districts during peak hours, and tailor response teams to the dominant offense types in each area.
